🔥 Hardest Questions — Concise Answers
Q: Why this project specifically?
Crypto trading is a ₹multi-trillion market where 80% of retail traders lose money due to emotion and information overload. AI removes both. Crypto also trades 24/7 — no market hours — making it ideal for ML (more data).
Q: Why XGBoost/RF/LSTM — not better models?
These ARE state-of-the-art for tabular time-series. XGBoost wins most financial ML benchmarks. Transformers/GPT need 10× more data and compute — overkill for this scope. We cover all 3 ML families deliberately.
Q: What's unique about your approach?
1) Predict returns not price (statistically correct). 2) Include transaction costs in backtest (most don't). 3) Long AND Short positions. 4) Time-aware split (no data leakage). 5) Live signal on app load.
Q: Your accuracy doesn't look high — why?
We predict daily returns — extremely noisy data. In financial ML, 55%+ direction accuracy is considered strong and profitable. Even hedge funds rarely exceed 60%. 50% = coin flip. We beat that consistently.
Q: Why these 11 indicators?
Based on the base research paper (Entropy 2020). They cover all 4 signal categories: trend (SMA, WMA), momentum (RSI, MOM, MACD), oscillator (Stochastic, Williams %R, CCI), volume (A/D Line). Validated by XGBoost feature importance.
Q: Why 14 days for most indicators?
Industry standard established by J. Welles Wilder (RSI inventor) in 1978. Balances sensitivity (too short = noise) vs lag (too long = misses moves). Used by Bloomberg, Reuters, all major trading platforms.
Q: Calendar goes to 2015 but memory is 1–60 days — why?
Completely different parameters. Date range = training dataset size (years of learning). Sequence length = context window per prediction (lines read before answering). Like: study 10 years of textbooks, read last 10 lines before each answer.
Q: Is your backtest realistic?
Yes — three reasons. 1) Time-aware split: test data is always the most recent 20% (never shuffled). 2) 0.1% transaction cost per trade entry. 3) Both Long and Short positions. Most academic backtests skip all three.

📏 Metrics — What Every Number Means
RMSE
Lower = better
Average prediction error. Targets are daily returns (tiny decimals like 0.002), so RMSE of 0.000X is normal and expected.
Direction Accuracy (DA)
>55% = good
% of days AI predicted UP/DOWN correctly. 50% = coin flip. Most important metric for trading. We don't need exact price — just direction.
Sharpe Ratio
>1.0 = good
Return ÷ Risk, annualised × √365 (crypto = 365 days). Like salary-to-stress ratio. >2 = excellent. Used by every professional fund.
Max Drawdown
Lower = safer
Worst peak-to-trough drop. If portfolio hit ₹15k then fell to ₹9k — drawdown = 40%. Professional target: <20%.
Win Rate
>50% = useful
% of active trades (signal ≠ HOLD) that were profitable. Only counts when AI actually took a position — not Hold days.
Why not MAPE?
Division by zero
MAPE divides by actual value. Daily returns near zero → division by ~0 → infinite/meaningless output. DA is more honest.
